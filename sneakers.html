ADIDAS YEEZY 700 V3 CLAY BROWN

The third generation of the Yeezy 700 series has a very different from the look of the v1 and v2 700s. Instead of the “dad shoe” trend, it follows the futuristic direction, which is mostly due to the “Alien-like” cage running across the entire side of the sneaker. There are however elements that link to previous generations, such as the characteristic stitching on the upper that has already debuted on the v2s. An important change is that for some reason Kanye didn’t include Boost soles for this sneaker.

ADIDAS YEEZY BOOST 350 V2 LIGHT

The most popular model in Kanye’s YEEZY series is clearly the Yeezy Boost 350 v2. Its success has been unbroken since the first Beluga was released. Dozens of colorways have come to light since then: versions with SPLY350 inscriptions and transparent stripes, pull-tabs and no pull-tabs. One detail that has always remained the same is the the comfortable Boost sole and flexible Primeknit upper.

ADIDAS YEEZY 350 V2 BELUGA REFLECTIVE

In September 2016 the first Yeezy Boost 350 v2 colourway named "Beluga" was released. The collaboration of Adidas and Kanye Yeezy exploded. Needless to say, the hype around the Yeezy 750 or 350 v1 had already been on fire, but the 350 v2 elevated this collab to a different level. While Kanye expressed his goal “YEEZYs for everyone”, more and more colour combos came to light. The popularity of the sneaker with its comfortable Boost sole and Primeknit upper is still undiminished.

ADIDAS YEEZY 500 CLAY BROWN

The Yeezy 500 was quite form-breaking at the time of their first release, not only because of the Adi-Prene sole technology but also the bold combination of mesh and split leather elements made it stand out from the average sneaker. Due to the use of materials and colors, this silhouette creates a less sporty effect providing possibilities to wear Yeezys in different styles and aesthetics.

ADIDAS YEEZY FOAM RUNNER MXT MOON GREY

An evolutionary step in the world of sneakers? The futuristic-looking Yeezy Foam RNNR debuted in the summer of 2020, and that couldn't be taken lightly either. It's not easy to categorise either, perhaps it can be seen as a shoe-slide hybrid, but its main aim is maximum comfort. It's a one-piece shoe made of EVA foam mixed with algae - making the manufacturing process more environmentally friendly.

ADIDAS YEEZY BOOST 700 FADED AZURE

Kanye West’s adidas Yeezy’s 700 silhouette played a big role in triggering the “dad shoe” or “chunky sneaker” trend, thanks to the thick and comfortable Boost sole structure, and the top made of many panels. Reminiscent of the lines of the 90s, the style of this sneaker can be spotted immediately.

ADIDAS YEEZY QNTM BARIUM

The Yeezy QNTM is a basketball style sneaker designed for streetwear from the collaboration between Kanye West and adidas. Comfort is provided by adidas Boost technology, and style is provided by the fact that it's a Yeezy: the details feature styling cues from the Yeezy 700 v3 and Yeezy 380. As high-tops are a rarity in Yeezy models these days, whether you're playing basketball or wearing it for colder weather, it's a good choice!

ADIDAS YEEZY SLIDE BONE

The Yeezy Slide slipper is completely clean, consisting of practically a single piece of EVA material. The toothed outsole is also a design element, with branding including the three-striped adidas logo below the heel. This model has also played a role in making the slipper a sterling wear on the street!
